We want everyone to understand limits in plain language—no telecom jargon required.

## The idea in one sentence

Famulor enforces a **daily outbound quota only for calls that leave through our integrated (shared) carrier**. Traffic on **your own SIP trunk**, **inbound** PSTN traffic, and **browser-based web calls** are **not** subject to that same daily cap.

Your workspace’s live counters and renewal time are under **[Limits](https://app.famulor.de/limits)**.

![Limits overview in the Famulor app](/images/limits-outboundcalls.png)

## What the integrated-carrier daily quota is

**Integrated carrier** means: Famulor provisions PSTN connectivity for you (for example a number from us plus routing over our built-in provider). For that path, outbound calls draw from a **shared daily pool** per workspace so the platform stays fair and predictable.

- **Allowance** — the maximum number of those integrated outbound calls in the current period (often around **1,000 per day**; your workspace may differ).
- **Usage** — how many of those calls you have already placed in the period; **remaining** is allowance minus usage.
- **Renewal** — the quota rolls forward on a **rolling ~24 hour** basis; the moment of renewal is per account and is the one Famulor applies for enforcement.

**What counts toward the quota**

- Outbound calls from **numbers supplied by Famulor** on the integrated path.
- Outbound calls where **your own number appears as caller ID**, when that setup still goes out through the **integrated** carrier (you are using our trunk while presenting your CLI).

**What does not count**

Anything that does **not** use that integrated outbound trunk for PSTN—see the next section.

## What stays outside the daily outbound quota

These categories are **not** limited by Famulor’s integrated daily outbound cap:

- **Inbound PSTN calls** — calls **to** your assistant’s number are not debited against integrated outbound allowance.
- **Outbound via your SIP trunk** — signaling and media go to **your** carrier (Twilio, Telnyx, Zadarma, DIDLogic, etc.). Famulor does not apply its integrated **per-day outbound** cap there; your carrier’s own limits and billing apply.
- **Web calls** — sessions started in the **browser** (embeddable widget or public demo) use a different access pattern than “dial out over our integrated PSTN trunk,” so they are **not** tallied like integrated outbound for this quota.

## Need more outbound capacity? Send a request

Yes. If your volume grows and you need a **higher daily limit** on the **integrated** trunk, use **Request increase** in the dashboard (linked from **[Limits](https://app.famulor.de/limits)**). A short form opens—we use your answers to review the change quickly.

You’ll be asked for:

- **Requested daily limit** — the number of outbound calls per day you’re aiming for (required).
- **Why you need a higher limit** — a few sentences about your use case (required). Honest context helps us say yes faster.
- **Business website** — optional, but useful if we want to learn how you use Famulor.

Then choose **Submit request** or **Cancel**.

![Request Outbound Limit Increase form](/images/limits-request-increase.png)

We review requests manually. If something is unclear, we may reach out through your usual support channel.

## Prefer no daily cap on outbound?

Connecting **your own SIP trunk** means outbound PSTN is originated by **your** carrier. Famulor’s integrated **daily outbound** quota no longer applies to that traffic; you keep the same **per-minute AI** usage model while voice minutes are between you and the carrier. Setup is guided in the product, or start in the docs: [SIP integration](/en/provisioning/sip-ai/sip-integration).

<Tip>
Allowance, usage, and renewal time are **per workspace**. The values under [app.famulor.de/limits](https://app.famulor.de/limits) are what Famulor enforces.
</Tip>
